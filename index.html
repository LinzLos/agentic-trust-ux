Note 2 · Attestation Shield
Decision
Lead with a plain-language verdict; keep the raw TEE quote one click away.
Why
Hardware guarantees are invisible. A non-technical owner needs a glanceable verdict; a protocol engineer needs the actual quote to audit. Same proof, two audiences.
Trade-off
Abstraction risks "trust theater." Mitigated by making the real cryptographic proof always reachable, never hidden.

Note 3 · Activity
Decision
Show intent ("what it's doing"), not logs. The raw trace lives behind a toggle.
Why
This is the role's core question — visualize background agent work without overwhelming. Intent matches the human's mental model; logs are the machine's.
Trade-off
Summaries can hide edge cases, so the raw-log toggle is the escape hatch for power users and debugging.

Note 1 · Trust Dial
Decision
Trust is set per action with one moving threshold — not granted per app at install.
Why
Users over-grant at install and never revisit. A single legible dial keeps consent live and adjustable.
Trade-off
One global threshold is coarse; v2 adds per-category limits (DeFi vs. data vs. compute).

Design stance
  • Legibility over logs. Intent first; the raw trace is one click away, never the default.
  • Intervenability over automation-only. Pause, cap, and override mid-flight — always.
  • Glanceable but auditable. The Shield reads in a second; the proof is there for the skeptic.
  • Web2 ease, Web3 sovereignty. The keys stay yours; the friction scales with the stakes.
